Platform Pillars

Each pillar distinguishes what can be executed through SBA vs. what requires administrative action.
Pillar 1

Networking & LL.M.–J.D. Integration

Networking should not depend on luck. It must be engineered.

Deliverables ▾
  • SBALL.M.–J.D. mentor pairing with monthly touchpoints and tracked participation.
  • SBASpeed-networking mixers every 6 weeks (structured rotations + follow-up matching).
  • SBAGlobal Expertise Registry (countries/languages/practice areas) available to students and clinics.
  • ADMINCareer-fair lane for international/cross-border profiles and employers who actually interview them.
Pillar 2

Bar Readiness & a Known Path

LL.M. bar success must be repeatable, not “mystery-based.”

Deliverables ▾
  • SBATexas bar roadmap for foreign-trained candidates: timeline, course planning, MPRE, and resources.
  • SBAWeekly accountability groups for practice essays and MBE-style questions.
  • ADMINLL.M. bar-skills series during the academic year (practice built into the schedule).
  • ADMINAnnual anonymized outcomes report (what prep worked, what students would do differently).
Pillar 3

Professional Brand & Employer Value

Stop framing LL.M. students as “junior JDs.” Start framing them as cross-border assets.

Deliverables ▾
  • SBAEmployer-ready resume & LinkedIn templates tailored to foreign-trained experience.
  • SBATexas-focused employer roundtables: what firms want, how they evaluate LL.M. candidates, and what gets interviews.
  • ADMINStructured access to DFW/Texas employers with cross-border practices and in-house needs.

Pillar 4

Interview Prep That Fits LL.M. Reality

Generic interview prep misses visa questions, translation of experience, and U.S. law-firm expectations.

Deliverables ▾
  • SBALL.M. mock interview bank with actual question patterns (visa, “why LL.M.”, practice differences).
  • SBARecorded mock interviews with structured feedback and improvement rubrics.
  • ADMINAlumni-led interview clinics with attorneys who supervise and hire.
Pillar 5

Immigration & Work Authorization Clarity

Confusion causes missed deadlines and missed opportunities. We need one clear playbook.

Deliverables ▾
  • SBASingle LL.M. immigration guide: CPT/OPT basics, timelines, and employer conversations.
  • ADMINJoint semester workshops with international office + career services + guest immigration counsel.
Pillar 6

Aggie Network Activation for LL.M.

The network exists. The gap is access and routing — we will build routing.

Deliverables ▾
  • SBAMonthly “Aggie Introductions” program: curated alumni conversations in Texas markets.
  • SBASector nights (in-house, energy, tax, immigration, compliance) driven by employer demand.
  • ADMINIdentify alumni/employers with a history of interviewing international candidates.
Pillar 7

Specialization Signal (Even in a General LL.M.)

If employers cannot read your focus in 10 seconds, you do not have a focus.

Deliverables ▾
  • SBAPre-built course maps for Texas-relevant tracks (business/compliance, litigation/bar, tech/privacy).
  • ADMINMicro-credentials or certificates that signal competence to employers.
New Proposal

LL.M. Representatives Must Have a Vote

Representation without voting power is symbolic. LL.M. reps should vote on procedural and substantive SBA matters.

Deliverables ▾
  • SBABylaw amendment to grant LL.M. representatives full voting rights.
  • SBAPublish meeting outcomes and vote records to ensure accountability.
  • ADMINRequest formal endorsement of equal voting rights as an integration commitment.
