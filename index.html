About The Candidate

I am a licensed Colombian attorney with 5 years of experience, a Master's in Taxation, and an IRS Enrolled Agent credential. Currently pursuing my LL.M. at Texas A&M, I am running for SBA Representative to bridge the gap between our programs and ensure every student has the tools to succeed in the global market.

My Platform

Comprehensive Solutions for Networking, Academics, and Career Success

1. Networking & LL.M.-J.D. Integration

Mentorship Program

Launch a peer-mentor system pairing LL.M.s with J.D. students for co-hosting study groups, social mixers, and mutual class shadowing.

Speed Networking

Organize "International Speed Networking" events where J.D.s and LL.M.s rotate through short conversations to exchange cultural and legal insights.

Jurisdictional Registry

Create a database of LL.M. alumni specialties and home countries, allowing J.D. students and firms to easily find experts in specific global markets.

Shared Student Orgs

Facilitate active LL.M. membership in journals, clinics, and the Board of Advocates to co-sponsor cultural nights and moot courts.

2. Bar Exam Preparation & Support

Bar-Track Curriculum

Introduce an optional "Bar-Track" certificate with weekly practice classes, MBE drills, and essay workshops integrated into the schedule.

Dedicated Advising

Assign faculty advisors experienced in bar rules to help students map out registration deadlines and required subject courses early.

Workshops & Prep

Host Spring/Fall workshops on test-taking strategies and subsidize commercial prep courses (e.g., Themis, BARBRI) for LL.M. students.

Alumni Study Groups

Form inter-class study groups where past test-takers mentor current students, utilizing data on past LL.M. pass rates to set realistic targets.

3. Professional Brand & Career Services

"Global Lawyer" Badge

Establish certificates for specialized skills (e.g., Business Law, Compliance) to help LL.M.s market themselves as experts to employers.

Resume Coaching

Provide specialized training on how to frame bilingualism and civil-law expertise as a "window to the world" for U.S. firms.

Employer Engagement

Host panels with international trade firms and corporate counsel who value foreign-educated lawyers, demystifying the hiring path.

4. Recruiting & Interview Prep

Targeted Fairs (ISIP)

Partner with consortiums to send qualified TAMU students to the International Student Interview Program (ISIP) in NYC.

Mock Interviews

Organize mock interviews with alumni who were former LL.M. hires to practice answering questions about foreign legal backgrounds.

On-Campus Recruiting

Create an "International Track" at TAMU career fairs, inviting firms specifically looking for H-1B candidates or foreign language skills.

5. Visa Support & The Aggie Network

Visa Transparency

Publish clear flowcharts for CPT/OPT timelines and host workshops with immigration attorneys to explain H-1B and TN visa odds.

Staff Training

Ensure all career counselors are trained on basic immigration rules to provide accurate advice on internships and work authorization.

Alumni Connections

Organize happy hours with the DFW and Houston Aggie Law alumni chapters to connect students with practicing attorneys.

Dual-Degree Options

Explore fast-track J.D./LL.M. pathways for students who wish to deepen their U.S. legal training and marketability.
